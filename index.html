Every Trap Looks Fine in the Proposal.
Here Is Where They Hide.

Two proposals can look similar on price while offering very different levels of protection. Compare what is documented before work begins, how the project is managed and who remains accountable after completion.

Written Warranty

Written vs Verbal Warranty

The trap: Most warranties are spoken, never signed. When the surface fails, there is nothing on paper, and the phone stops getting answered.

With WLP: Qualifying work includes up to 15 years of written warranty, with terms, conditions and responsibilities defined before you sign. If we build it wrong, we pay to fix it, so we build it right the first time.

Scope Clarity

Scope Clarity vs Hidden Costs

The trap: A vague scope is a blank check. Thin asphalt, skipped prep and “unforeseen” change orders appear right after your deposit clears.

With WLP: Your Paving Advisor documents the full scope before work begins: prep, materials, quantities, exclusions and responsibilities. No surprises, no “that was not included.”

Conditions

Conditions That Work for You vs Rigged Against You

The trap: Some contractors steer you toward what is easiest for them to sell, not what your pavement needs, then bury the fine print that voids the warranty.

With WLP: We confirm what qualifies and what is required upfront. If corrective work is needed to qualify, it is on the proposal before construction, not discovered later.

Accountability

One Accountable Expert vs a Vanishing Salesperson

The trap: Once you sign, the salesperson disappears. You chase a crew that answers to no one for updates, approvals or changes.

With WLP: One dedicated Paving Advisor stays from review to completion. A paving expert whose job is to solve your problem, not to close a sale.

Scheduling

Work Around Your Property vs Around Their Schedule

The trap: Volume crews block your entrances, ignore your tenants and rush the finish, damaging the surface they just laid.

With WLP: We phase the work around your operations, traffic and business hours, protecting your tenants and the quality of the installation.

Project Record

A Permanent Record vs a Contractor Who Ghosts You

The trap: When the lot is striped, they are gone. No documentation, no record, no one to call when a covered issue shows up.

With WLP: You get your written warranty, a full digital project record, a video proposal and a project documentary you can attach to your property file for resale and value.

What Everyone Asks After a Bid Goes Wrong

Look for four red flags: no written warranty, a scope you cannot itemize line by line, no named point of contact, and pressure to decide today. Any one of them means the risk is being moved onto you. All four together means the price you are quoted is not the price you will pay.

Because the savings come out of the parts you cannot see. Base preparation, material thickness and compaction are where a bid gets cut, and none of them show on day one. They show in year three, when the surface fails and the repair costs more than the difference you saved.

A promise you cannot hold in your hand is not a warranty. Ask for the term, the conditions, what voids it and who is responsible for what, in writing, before you sign. Ours is documented and enforceable, which is why it is on paper before construction starts.
